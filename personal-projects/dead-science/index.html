Dead Science

Unknown Outcome

Dead Science is a first person exploration and puzzles game in which the player takes the rol of a woman who investigates a case of the Blair Family's disappearance. It is a science fiction story with a progressive complexity on its puzzle, with a main mechanic of a visor which allows the playuer to explore other dimensions. All the game has a realistic aesthetic using all the power of Unreal Engine 4, with simple controls to make it more accessible to the public and with a short duration.

In this project I had several positions. My main job was Producer of the Unknown Outcome university team. In this position, I was in charge of supervising the development, controlling the times of each member, organizing, schematizing and assigning each task and writing all this information weeek after week. We work thorugh the Scrum methodology adapted to our position as university students.

In this project I also worked as gameplay designer, level designer, mechanic and puzzle designer, as well as screenwriter. I was in charge of writing all the necessary documentation so that the artist and programmers could work, detailing all the element of the scene, the interaction with it as well as puzzles and gameflow. In addition, I was responsible for the creation of whiteboxes in UE4.

On the other hand, I worked as gameplay, mechanics and UI programmer. I worked in part on the interaction with objects and items, procedural generation of modular elements and user experience. These tasks were developed in UE4 through Blueprints, althoug I am currently converting it to C++.

Also I worked as Business and Public Relations Developer, taking Dead Science to different events to make it known to the public, arranging meetings with publishers in order to obtain funciond and promoting it in the different social networks of the studio.

Dead Science appeared in some of the most important videogame events in Spain such as Madrid Games Week, Fun&Serious or IndieMAD.
