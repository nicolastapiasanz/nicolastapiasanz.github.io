Simulation in VR

OED (Observatorio Español del Diseño)

This was an investigation project I did in my third year at my university, ESNE inside of the OED (Observatorio Español del Diseño, Spanish Design Observatory), in collaboration with Lenovo, DGT (Dirección General de Tráfico, General Direction of Traffic), INSIA (Instituto Universitario de Investigación del Automovil de la UPM, UPM Universitary Institute of Car Investigation) and TASI (Transportation Active Safety Institute). The objective of this investigation was to create real scenes of running over cars to study, in VR, the behaviour of different subjects in the same situations. With this, it was intended to predict the possible movement that a pedestrian would do to implement it on the future automatic braking systems.

For the development of this experience were used two different type of VR headsets: Lenovo Explorer and HTC Vive Pro. Among my different tasks, I had to investigate the use of Lenovo Explorer and all the Microsoft Windows Mixed Reality environment in Unity. On the other side, I developed a tool to generate different pedestrians for the scene to give some facilities for the designer.

Also, I did some optimizations on the 3D models in Unity using Simplygon to make more efficient the experience in VR.
